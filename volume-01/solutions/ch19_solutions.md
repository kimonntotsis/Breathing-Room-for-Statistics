# Chapter 19 Solutions

**E19.1** The patient contributed follow-up time but did not have an event by day 365; their time is used up to censoring.

**E19.2** When baseline hazards differ or absolute risk matters more than relative hazard; also when events are sparse and CI is wide.

**E19.3** It compares survival curves, detecting whether event times differ between groups overall.

**E19.4** HR alone hides event rate and precision; sparse events can yield unstable HRs with misleadingly narrow appearance if counts are omitted.

**E19.5** When follow-up differs between patients or timing of events matters; Cox uses full timeline until censoring.
