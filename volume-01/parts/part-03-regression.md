# Part III: Regression (continuous, binary, count) {.unnumbered}

## In the room: the exacerbation endpoint

The protocol labels exacerbations as a **count**, but the first draft manuscript reports logistic “any exacerbation” because the PI prefers odds ratios. Mei stops the thread: *“Pick the estimand, then the GLM family.”* Part III is that discipline — adjusted associations on the right outcome scale, without stepwise fishing (Ch 7).

---

**This part covers:** Gaussian models for continuous outcomes (Ch 5), GLMs for binary and count endpoints (Ch 6), and prespecified model building (Ch 7). The goal is not “run a model” but state the estimand, fit defensibly, and report estimate + uncertainty + limits.

**Read this if:** your outcome is continuous, binary, or count and you need adjusted associations.

**Skip this if:** you only need an unadjusted arm comparison (Part II, Ch 4) or a prediction model with validation metrics (Part IV, Ch 9).
