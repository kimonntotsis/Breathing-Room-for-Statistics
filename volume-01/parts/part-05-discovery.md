# Part V: Structure Discovery (many markers) {.unnumbered}

Unsupervised methods are where overclaiming is most common in respiratory research.

This part teaches a defensible discovery workflow:

1. **Reduce dimensions** (Ch 10) to visualize and stabilize noisy marker panels
2. **Cluster defensibly** (Ch 11): stability, batch checks, endotype claim ladder
3. **Integrate end-to-end** (Ch 12): write publication-style narratives and say what was *not* proven

Ch 10 and Ch 11 include “alternatives menus” so you can choose methods based on marker type (continuous, binary, mixed), dimensionality (p>>n), and goal (exploration vs supervised reduction).
